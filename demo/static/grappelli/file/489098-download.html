Colloids

Colloids or colloidal solution are heterogeneous mixtures. The particles of a colloid are uniformly distributed throughout the solution. However, the particle size is too small to be seen with the naked eye, but is big enough to scatter light. eg. Milk

Due to smaller size of particles as compared to that of a suspension, Milk appears to be homogeneous.

The components of a colloidal solution are the dispersed phase and the dispersion medium.

The solute-like component or the dispersed particles in a colloid form the dispersed phase. The component in which the dispersed phase is suspended is known as the dispersing medium.

Colloids are classified according to the state (solid, liquid or gas) of the dispersing medium and the dispersed phase.
